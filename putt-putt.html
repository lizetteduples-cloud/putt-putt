📖 The Rules of Putt-Putt

Fair, friendly, and fiercely competitive 😄

🎯
The goal: get your ball in the hole in as few strokes as possible. Lowest total score wins the game.
1️⃣
Order of play: on hole 1, play in any agreed order. After that, the player with the best score on the previous hole tees off first — that's called "honours". Keep the rest of the order the same.
✋
6-stroke limit: if you haven't holed out after 6 strokes, pick up your ball and write down a 7. This keeps the game moving (and saves friendships 😅).
🧱
Ball stuck against a wall or obstacle? You may move it one putter-head length away — no penalty.
🚀
Ball leaves the course: place it back where it went out and add one penalty stroke.
⏸️
Wait for the ball to stop completely before playing your next stroke. No hockey-style dribbling!
👟
Etiquette: don't stand in another player's putting line, keep quiet during their stroke, and don't step on the green part of the hole while someone plays.
🕳️
Finish every hole before moving on — everyone holes out (or hits the 6-stroke limit), then you move to the next hole together.
⭐
Hole-in-one: score it as 1 and celebrate loudly. This app will celebrate with you 🎉
🤝
Ties: if totals are equal at the end, the player with the most holes-in-one wins. Still tied? Play a sudden-death hole!
😄
The golden rule: it's putt-putt — have fun. The windmill hole is rigged for everyone equally.
